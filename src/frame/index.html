Anchor · Position

Frame

The umbrella sits inside a stance. Below: the four positions WULD's work assumes — each named with its canonical academic referent, then situated through the specific inflection that distinguishes this body of work from the broader literature. Not a glossary; the Glossary is for coined vocabulary. This page is for the stance everything else operates within.

Negative utilitarianism

Canonical: minimize suffering rather than maximize pleasure

WULD's inflection — awaiting chat-side authorship. What does the asymmetry between suffering-prevention and pleasure-creation look like inside this body of work? Where does this position part company with mainstream NU readings (Smart, Mayerfeld, etc.)?

Anti-natalism

Canonical: procreation is morally impermissible (Benatar et al.)

WULD's inflection — awaiting chat-side authorship. Where does this body of work stand on the asymmetry argument, the consent objection, the misanthropic vs. philanthropic AN distinction? What's the specific register of pessimism the umbrella inherits from this position?

Structural pessimism

Canonical: the bad outweighs the good as a feature of existence, not a contingent fact

WULD's inflection — awaiting chat-side authorship. What's the version of structural pessimism this work argues from? Schopenhauer? Mainländer? Ligotti? Or a different lineage entirely? Where does the umbrella's reading depart from the academic-canonical positions?

Voluntary human extinction

Canonical: the species should cease procreating, as a moral conclusion

WULD's inflection — awaiting chat-side authorship. How does this body of work treat VHE as conclusion vs. position-among-others? What's the relationship to efilism (per the argument library when stable tag lands), to the broader extinctionist literature, to the EVF/VHEMT distinction?
