Glossary · N

Ne Hoc Fiat

Pos. phr. (Lat.) Etymology Lat. ne + hoc + fiat First use WULD editorial canon
Size 100%

Definition

Latin: let this not be done. A refusal-formula carried across the body of the work — what the modal-architectural pessimist would have said before the structure committed itself to manufacture. Not a wish, because the wishing-class would have been a feature of the structure, and the formula’s whole weight is that no such feature was in place. Not a prayer, because prayers presuppose a listener. Ne Hoc Fiat is the form a protest takes when the protester knows in advance that the formula will land nowhere — that nothing in the architecture is positioned to receive it, terminate it, or honor it. The formula speaks the asymmetry: a refusal is grammatically possible, but the grammar has no audience.

(The book in progress at /ne-hoc-fiat/ takes this formula as its working title and its thesis.)

Etymology & register

Classical Latin subjunctive of negation. Ne — negative particle in prohibition clauses. Hoc — “this.” Fiat — third-person singular present subjunctive of fieri, “to be done / to become.” Standard Latin construction; non-standard philosophical deployment. The deployment is exact: the subjunctive carries the speaker’s wish-without-power, which is the exact emotional shape the position requires.
