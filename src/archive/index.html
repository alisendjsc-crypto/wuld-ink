Archive

A discrete cabinet of disparate objects — clinical treatise next to baroque scream next to a noise-wall. Curated to show range, not completeness.

The answer lies in the concept of defiant archiving and metacognitive exploration. Works like Love Void Love: Writings of Things and Other Things, The Point, The Book of Nought, and The Known and the Unknown serve as meticulous records of existential crisis. The Point, specifically, is noted as having been written during a period of intense mental health struggle, encompassing severe panic attacks and non-epileptic-psychogenic-pseudo-seizures during the pandemic (circa 2021). The act of writing, therefore, becomes a crucial mechanism for metacognition—thinking about how and why we think, and observing the breakdown of the mind from a third-party perspective. It is an attempt to systematically map the boundaries of the void and document the failure of human consciousness. — Self-frame

Book · Dec 2021 · The philosophical pass

The Point

Numbered-fragment metaphysical nihilism, written through severe panic attacks and non-epileptic-psychogenic-pseudo-seizures during the pandemic (circa 2021).

Non-existence = default because void remains void because void = void as opposed to the notion of existence or ‘things themselves’ (i.e. non illusory independences of observance). Existence remains just as undefined as non-existence, whereas non-existence = at least logically deductible through a series of questions and analysis: What is a thing fundamentally, to the very core? How? And if you cannot answer these questions without postulations of infinity somethings or ‘errors in the universe’ the answer will always = a void remains a void because void = void, logically, necessarily, absolutely, completely, totally, utterly and entirely.

With my acknowledgment of w-holes (replacement word for ‘things’) I must admit that our perceptual foundation of the world through the scientific method = undeniable in a concrete (supraliminal) sense. W-holes do not rely on my belief to = w-hole. I am however less and less certain of the due repercussions of my original positions. Have I really said anything new and original, or have I just reinvented language a little bit (flipped words upside down)? I can at least say with some certainty that I can present a very good game to play.

— The Point

In regards to the “logical narrative”, of course, I understand that this is all anfractuous aporia — blatant — and tortuous impasse of “paradox”. It is itself. It means itself. That will never be untrue, but what it means, cannot be explicated ulteriorly as a universal impossibility. This is the unannouncing non-existence of intelligence. This is the profundity of knowing absolutely nothing. This is the limitation of everything. It is not the prevention or the inception or the termination, but it isn’t what it isn’t all the more.

— The Point

Book · 2022 · The affective pass

Hatred

Single sustained baroque scream; near-silent release.

Hatred is a tough feeling to overcome; the most resilient to change; the most inhospitable to any exemption of guilt or shame; a drought to the soul, that makes skin crawl, underneath blood boiling with veins akin to barb-wire – strangling the heart, an already asphyxiated muscle, unrelenting to the ash of history – pursuing like mad, fountains of saliva and sweat from every orifice, betrayal and self-deceit. Consciousness cannot be enough; the mind-eye of our whirling, whirring hour – scraping out of the wounds so sour – imagining a necessary escape through the portal and recess of what is. Monotony and reference to what isn’t; repetitive motion; memory, nepotism and sameness – nescience, fear, estrangement, and annihilation.

— Hatred

The Known and the Unknown

Itself a compendium: Void Love · Mementos · Esoteric Writings · Illustrations.

The empty room

Known and the Unknown.

“What.” or more aptly put “.”, was the way I felt best described my interpretation of my own original philosophy — “Existence is impossible and will always be impossible. There is simply no-thing. Nothing. Naught at all.

“A void remains a void because it is a void.

“There was never anything, because there was no-thing to start with. (If a thing requires a thing to begin or exist, then there cannot be a first thing to start with by rule of ‘cause and effect’, else you argue the eternal (non-observable), infinite and imperceptible by logic and eye (‘proof’ by sense).

“The universal and ostensibly insoluble question ‘why does everything exist?’ cannot and will never be righteously answered, because it doesn’t exist.”

— Behold, Grey Incantation (JSC) · The Known and the Unknown

‘Real-World’-Principles of Existence [Reality]—Ontology:

1. Logic is how you describe and not the description itself.
2. The description itself is a representation of things themselves [depending, though-however, on whether these icons are not merely synthetic inventions of confusion or deliberate imagination].
3. Words are symbols [‘pointers’, so to speak, ideally] and not abstract-arbitrary creations [projections] of things themselves.
4. Autogenesis [of originality, redundancy, similarity and ‘true’ randomness] is non-guaranteed.
5. Non-guaranteed means no prior determining factors.
6. Void of reason does not equal prevention [limitation or restriction of inception—beginning of things themselves—from the void, literally], however, since logic is non-intrinsic [reference to above statement (1.)].
6-2. Disappearance [deletion] is by definition illogical (without explanation), since dissipation and transmutation (solution-dissolution) are only categorized thoroughly through things themselves.
7. A thing is what it is and no ‘other’ [other thing—aside from itself] by its own nature.
7-2. Things are what they are by their own definition.
8. Inherent qualia [things themselves], beginnings and ends only come from things themselves [and or no-thing; deductible-traceable to an ultimate, essential origin of nothingness—non-thing—void].
9. There is no ultimate, universal thing if a thing is not existent by its own nature.

— The Known and the Unknown · Esoteric Writings

Book · 2019 · Folded into K&U · The personal-fragment substratum

Love Void Love · Mementos

The rawest material. One pull, no more — the form closes the loop.

It’s not your fault if you hate. It’s not your fault if you do not love. It’s not okay if we are blamed for not wanting to live. It’s okay to admit that it’s not okay. I have nowhere to go. I want to leave. I am not at home. I am not in peace. I do not love. I hate. I do not want to live. I love anyone who would help me. I would love anyone I have helped. I accept you, if you accept me. I want to believe you are more than I perceive—caring and considerate—completely non-ignorant; so stand up and kick it, please—kill me, kill me, kill me. Void love void.

— Love Void Love
